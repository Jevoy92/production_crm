# Palmer House Investigative Series Map

## The Thesis

Palmer House Productions investigates what happens when humans and cameras collide. The core question that unifies everything: **"What does the camera do to us?"**

This is not a video tips channel. This is investigative journalism about the psychology, history, and consequences of being recorded — with business application emerging naturally from the evidence.

## The Six Series

### Series A — "Before the Lens"
**6 episodes — The origin story of the recording impulse**

The premise: humans have been trying to record themselves since before language. Cave paintings, death masks, portraiture, daguerreotypes, home movies, selfies — it's all the same impulse expressed through different technology. This series traces that impulse from its first expression to its current form.

| Episode | Title | Core Investigation |
|---------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | The First Selfie | Cave handprints at Cueva de las Manos (7,300 BCE). Not art — identity. The first "I was here." |
| 2 | The Death Mask | Egyptian, Roman, Victorian death masks. Recording the face before it disappears. The original "save." |
| 3 | The Portrait Sitting | Pre-photography portraiture as performance. Sitting still for hours. The original "be yourself on camera." |
| 4 | The First Photograph of a Human | Boulevard du Temple, 1838. A man getting his shoes shined. Accidentally immortal. |
| 5 | The Home Movie | 8mm, Super 8, VHS. When families became their own documentarians. What changed in the house. |
| 6 | The Front-Facing Camera | 2003 (Sony Ericsson Z1010) to 2010 (iPhone 4). The moment we could see ourselves seeing ourselves. |

**Hero visual:** A handprint. Return to a handprint at the end of every episode.
**Ladders into:** Jevoy Palmer (foundational thinking), Palmer House (depth play), MindYourBizniz (entrepreneurs as storytellers).

### Series B — "Witness"
**6 episodes — The camera as observer, accomplice, and accuser**

The premise: every camera in every room is making a moral choice. The observer effect doesn't just describe physics — it describes us. This series investigates what happens when recording becomes the act, not the document of an act.

| Episode | Title | Core Investigation |
|---------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | Why We Move the Camera at the Worst Part | The instinct to tilt, pan away, drop the phone at impact. Embodied empathy bleeding through the device. |
| 2 | The Schoolyard Fight, Recorded | Phones + bystander effect. Recording as witness replacing intervention. |
| 3 | Bodycams and the Watched Watchers | Police bodycam adoption — did it deliver accountability? (Washington DC study, 2017) |
| 4 | The Capitol Self-Documenters | January 6 livestreamers who incriminated themselves. Performance instinct beating survival instinct. |
| 5 | The Christchurch Stream | When atrocity becomes content. Platform response. Media and motive. (Handle with extreme care.) |
| 6 | The Smile-You're-On-Camera Sticker | Ring cams, Nest, doorbell footage as genre. The home as surveilled fortress. |

**Hero visual:** A phone screen tilted slightly off-axis. The deflection moment.
**Ladders into:** Jevoy Palmer (signature work), MindYourBizniz (philosophy show), potentially documentary spinoff under YourBoyJevoy.

### Series C — "The Last Frame"
**5 episodes — People whose final moments were recorded — and what they recorded**

The most emotional series. Done with reverence, it becomes signature work. Done sloppily, it becomes exploitation. The line: the subjects are the heroes, not the spectacle.

| Episode | Title | Core Investigation |
|---------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | 127 Hours of Footage | Aron Ralston, April 2003. Hi-8 messages to family during entrapment. The instinct to be witnessed before dying. |
| 2 | Grizzly Man | Timothy Treadwell, 13 summers with bears. The camera as barrier between man and his own danger. |
| 3 | The Pilot's Last Words | Black box recordings. Eastern 401 (1972) to Germanwings (2015). What dying people say when their job is technical. |
| 4 | The Lone Sailor | Donald Crowhurst, 1968-69. Falsified logs, tape recordings of his own unraveling. The recorded mind disintegrating. |
| 5 | The Found Survivor | Cases where recordings saved the recorder — found photos, journals, GoPro pings. The camera as guardian angel. |

**Hero visual:** An SD card. A tape cassette. A small camera buried in snow.
**Ladders into:** Jevoy Palmer (signature long-form), Palmer House (Spotlight pillar — premium documentary work).

### Series D — "Selfie Deaths"
**3 episodes — The most documented contradiction of our time**

Short, sharp, algorithm-friendly. The kind of investigation that travels. People share it. Recommended as the **first series to produce** for maximum early traction.

| Episode | Title | Core Investigation |
|---------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | The Statistics That Should Have Made Us Stop | Bansal et al. (2018): 250+ documented deaths 2011-2017. Carnegie Mellon tracking. Why cliffs, beaches, train tracks? |
| 2 | The Cognitive Override | Phone-as-frame blocks peripheral threat detection. The camera literally overrides survival instincts. |
| 3 | The Larger Question | The selfie death as literal version of a metaphorical thing — framing ourselves so persistently we miss what's in the room. |

**Hero visual:** Phone screen with yellow framing brackets overlaid on dangerous terrain.
**Ladders into:** Jevoy Palmer signature investigation. Most algorithmically viable series — these episodes will travel.

### Series F — "The Audience That Doesn't Exist Yet"
**4 episodes — How we perform for hypothetical future watchers**

The most philosophical series. About the strange psychological condition of imagining future audiences who shape current behavior. The most "Jevoy Palmer brain" series in the whole map.

| Episode | Title | Core Investigation |
|---------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | The Audience Inside Your Head | Cooley's "looking-glass self" (1902). We've been imagining audiences forever. The camera just gave the imagined audience a body. |
| 2 | The Future Boss / The Future Date / The Future Hiring Committee | Young people growing up assuming everything will be reviewed. The ambient hiring pressure of being thirteen. |
| 3 | The Posthumous Audience | Recordings we leave behind. Digital grief. Posthumous social media accounts. The dead can still be heard. |
| 4 | The Ghost in the Algorithm | TikTok's recommendation engine as a non-human audience that pretends to know you. Performing for the algorithm. |

**Hero visual:** A single empty chair. A microphone on a stand with no one behind it.
**Ladders into:** The central Jevoy Palmer thesis. This is the work that builds the body of thought.

## The Investigative Method (House Style)

1. **Find the specific moment first.** Not the concept. The moment. A clip, a story, a single image, a single line. Build outward from there.
2. **Name the mechanism.** Bring the named research, the dated study, the cited thinker. This separates investigation from hot take.
3. **Honor the counterargument.** Always. The skeptic gets dignity before they get dismantled.
4. **Refuse the easy moral.** Hold the contradiction. Show the camera as savior in one frame and weapon in the next. Resist resolution.
5. **End on a question, not a slogan.** The body of work compounds when the audience leaves each episode with a question they can't shake.
6. **Use objects.** Always at least one prop that argues. Never a prop that only decorates.
7. **Verse capstone.** Keep it. It is the load-bearing wall of the voice.
