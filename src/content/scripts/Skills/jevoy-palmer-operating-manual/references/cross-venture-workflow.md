# Cross-Venture Script Model — Production Workflow

## The Principle

One investigation becomes multiple outputs. Same brain, different rooms. The research is shared. The audience experience is not.

This is the 1-to-12 model applied at the brand level: one core theme, investigated once, then delivered through three distinct doorways — each with its own register, CTA, and audience contract.

## The Three Versions

Every core theme gets written as three full scripts:

| Version | Register | Audience | CTA Direction | Runtime |
|---------|----------|----------|---------------|---------|
| Jevoy Palmer | Philosophical, personal, discovery-frame | Entrepreneurs, builders, thinkers | "Follow for more" / "Stay close" | 9–11 min |
| Palmer House Productions | Strategic, business-facing, outcome-driven | Business owners who need video | "Book a strategy call" / "Palmer House can help" | 9–10 min |
| MindYourBizniz | Intimate, raw, emotional, slow-burn podcast | Entrepreneurs wrestling with visibility | "Stay close. There's more." | 14–16 min |

## The Script Formula (All Versions)

Every script, regardless of version, follows this skeleton:

1. **Felt Moment** — Start with something painfully recognizable. A scene the audience has lived inside.
2. **Name the Mechanism** — Psychology, science, research. Duval & Wicklund, Hawthorne, Goffman, Cooley. Cite it. Date it.
3. **Show the Object** — Prop, scene, metaphor, demonstration. Something physical that argues the point.
4. **Admit the Counterargument** — "Now, the skeptic is right about one thing…" Give the objection dignity before dismantling the weak part.
5. **Flip the Frame** — The core insight. The reframe that changes the whole conversation.
6. **Make It Practical** — "What this means for you…" (varies by version)
7. **CTA + Verse** — Version-specific call to action, then faith capstone (Genesis 1:27 or equivalent, read slowly, no music).

## Version-Specific Differences

### Jevoy Palmer Version
- **Opening:** Personal observation or dinner-table moment
- **Tone:** "I want to investigate this" — curiosity-driven
- **Props:** Demonstrated on camera with visual cues in brackets
- **Counterargument device:** "The skeptic" — intellectual framing
- **Practical section:** Frameworks, principles, identity questions
- **CTA:** Soft authority — "If that question lives inside you too, stay close"
- **Stage cues:** Full cinematic direction (camera angles, lighting, B-roll)
- **Music:** None until Chapter 5 flip, then score the insight

### Palmer House Productions Version
- **Opening:** The "founder's freeze" — a business owner trying to record
- **Tone:** "Here's what this costs your business" — strategic
- **Props:** Same objects, but framed as business tools
- **Counterargument device:** "The strongest objection" — respectful but firm
- **Practical section:** The four pillars (System, Reel, Evergreen, Spotlight), what Palmer House actually does
- **CTA:** Direct business — "Book a strategy call. It's a real conversation, not a sales call dressed up as one."
- **Stage cues:** Cinematic + crew visible in Chapter 6 (proof of production quality)
- **Music:** Same structure — none until the flip

### MindYourBizniz Version
- **Opening:** Room tone, mug set down, "I want to talk about something"
- **Tone:** "Let me sit with this" — intimate, slow
- **Props:** Described verbally only (no visual demonstration — audio format)
- **Counterargument device:** "The friend who pushes back" — warmer, more personal
- **Practical section:** Architecture, not discipline — "the room gets built quietly"
- **CTA:** Relational — "If that's the kind of conversation you've been needing, stay close"
- **Production cues:** Audio-only (room tone, pauses, one music bed, verse read slowly)
- **Music:** One sustained pad under the most emotional chapter only

## The Non-Negotiable Rules

1. **No cross-contamination.** A Palmer House script never mentions MindYourBizniz. A Jevoy Palmer script never pitches Palmer House services. The ventures discover each other through the person, not through the content.

2. **Same research, different framing.** The Duval & Wicklund study appears in all three versions of Script 1. But in Jevoy Palmer it's "here's what I found." In Palmer House it's "here's what this costs your business." In MindYourBizniz it's "here's why you froze and it wasn't your fault."

3. **Props that argue, not decorate.** Every prop must do one of these jobs: Reveal (show what is hidden), Simplify (make complex instantly graspable), Contrast (show difference between two choices), Prove (demonstrate the principle in real time), Anchor (give audience something they remember later).

4. **The skeptic gets dignity before they get dismantled.** Never straw-man the counterargument. Give it full marks where it's correct. Then show where it has less ground than it thinks.

5. **Verse capstone is the load-bearing wall.** It arrives last. It arrives quietly. It reframes everything that came before without announcing itself as religious content.

6. **Launch framing still applies.** Even in Palmer House business scripts: "Here's what we do" not "Here's what we've done for hundreds of clients." Honest about the stage.

## Production Sequence

When building a new theme across all three versions:

1. **Research first.** Find the mechanism, the study, the named researcher, the date. This is shared across all versions.
2. **Write Jevoy Palmer version first.** This is the philosophical foundation — the purest expression of the idea.
3. **Write Palmer House version second.** Translate the philosophy into business consequence and Palmer House solution.
4. **Write MindYourBizniz version last.** Slow it down, make it intimate, add the "friend who pushes back," expand the emotional chapter.
5. **Extract clip-worthy lines** from each version for social cuts.
6. **Note guest expansion options** for MindYourBizniz Part 2 episodes.
