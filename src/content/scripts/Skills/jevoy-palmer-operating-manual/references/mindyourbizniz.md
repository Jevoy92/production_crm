# MindYourBizniz — Venture Profile

## Identity

- **Stage:** Pre-launch (as of mid-2026)
- **What it is:** The podcast arm of Palmer House Productions — solo monologue conversations about the emotional, psychological, and identity-level reality of being a business owner who is also a public figure now
- **Positioning:** "The conversation under the conversation." Not business tips. Not content advice. The inner life of entrepreneurship — the cost of visibility, the cost of invisibility, and the strange unspoken tension of running something with your name on it.
- **Relationship to Palmer House:** MindYourBizniz is a Palmer House conversation. It lives under the Palmer House Productions umbrella but has its own audience, its own register, and its own distribution (podcast platforms, not YouTube).

## Format

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| Format | Solo podcast monologue |
| Runtime | 14–16 minutes per episode |
| Word count | ~2,400–2,600 words per script |
| Distribution | Spotify, Apple Podcasts, podcast platforms |
| Frequency | Weekly (Season 1 target) |
| Production | Light post — room tone, one music bed per episode, minimal editing |

## Production Rules

These are non-negotiable format constraints:

1. **Open and close on room tone only.** No music at the top. Mug set down. Breath. Room.
2. **Intro cue is short** — 7 seconds max, then drops out completely.
3. **One sustained music bed per episode only** — placed under the most personal/emotional stretch, then dropped before the practical turn.
4. **Pace: longer pauses than feels comfortable.** This is the "talk to one person" show, not the "energy reel" show. Don't compress silences in edit.
5. **Verse capstone** — read slowly, no music underneath. Faith arrives last, as a reframe, never announced.
6. **Clip-worthy lines section** in every script — 3-4 lines designed for social cuts.
7. **Host outro** — "Thanks for being here. MindYourBizniz is a Palmer House conversation. New episode next week."
8. **Guest expansion options** noted at the end of each script — who could come on for a Part 2.

## Voice Register

MindYourBizniz is Jevoy at his most intimate and conversational. Compared to other ventures:

| Venture | Register | Energy |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Jevoy Palmer | Philosophical, discovery-frame, cinematic | Medium-high — investigating |
| Palmer House Productions | Strategic, business-facing, outcome-driven | High — persuading |
| **MindYourBizniz** | **Intimate, raw, emotional, slow-burn** | **Low — sitting with** |
| YourBoyJevoy | Playful, experimental, artistic | Variable — creating |

Key voice markers for MindYourBizniz:
- "I want to take my time with it"
- "I don't think this gets talked about enough"
- "If you've lived inside that scene"
- "I'm not interested in selling you anything today. I'm interested in naming what just happened."
- More pauses, more room tone, more silence
- The "friend who pushes back" as the counterargument device (not "the skeptic" — warmer framing)
- Direct address: "you" singular, not "you all" or "people"

## CTA Style

MindYourBizniz CTAs are soft, relational, and never salesy:

- "If that's the kind of conversation you've been needing — stay close. There's more."
- "Send it to the one business owner you know who needs to hear it before they delete another draft."
- "We'll probably unpack this more next week."
- "The business problem is rarely just business. Sometimes it's fear wearing a LinkedIn blazer."

## Content Themes

MindYourBizniz investigates the emotional reality of entrepreneurship:

- The cost of visibility for business owners
- The cost of invisibility (the invoice that comes later)
- Identity vs. brand (when your name IS the business)
- The freeze (why capable people can't press record)
- The gap between in-person authority and on-camera presence
- Strategy as avoidance (planning forever, publishing never)
- The audience that doesn't exist yet (performing for imagined future judges)
- Silence as strategy vs. silence as fear
- Being seen unfinished (the vulnerability of building in public)
- The architecture of showing up (room, light, cadence, not courage)

## Relationship to Other Ventures

MindYourBizniz shares research and themes with the other ventures but delivers them differently:

- **Same investigation, different room.** The research is shared. The delivery is venture-specific.
- **No cross-linking in content.** A MindYourBizniz listener should never hear a Palmer House sales pitch or a YourBoyJevoy art reference in their feed.
- **The ventures discover each other through the person, not through the content.**

The pattern:
- Jevoy Palmer asks: "What does visibility do to identity?"
- Palmer House answers: "How do we help businesses show up clearly on video?"
- MindYourBizniz sits with: "Why do entrepreneurs emotionally resist being seen?"
- YourBoyJevoy experiments: "How can this become an artistic exploration?"
