01 · Brand Essence

A translation company.

Palmer House is a translation company that uses cameras and editing as tools. We turn invisible expertise into visible proof, one-time explanations into evergreen assets, and scattered ideas into cohesive video systems.

Video isn't the goal

It's the tool that gets the business to its goals. The system serves the outcome.

Built like a workshop

Not an assembly line. Every project starts with the client's unique pain points.

Story dictates the edit

Not the other way around. Transcript-first, narrative-led, ruthlessly clear.

Four pillars, eight Pals

Spotlight · Reel · Evergreen · System. Every project maps to one.

House rules · how we work

Rule 01

Lead with the problem solved, not the service sold

Never open with what we make. Open with what's broken.

Rule 02

If you've explained it twice, it should be a video

Repetition is a bug. Documentation on camera is the fix.

Rule 03

Build once, use every time

Evergreen beats one-off. The 1-to-12 model: one shoot, twelve pieces.

Rule 04

Hooks come from observation, never a listicle

First 1–3 seconds. Real moment, not a template.

Rule 05

Don't be inspirational — be useful

Save someone time with every sentence. Practical beats poetic.

Rule 06

Quiet confidence, not hype

Less volume, more weight. Especially in Spotlight work.

Rule 07

Launch-stage framing only

Never claim "proven system" or "years of experience" credentials not yet earned.

04 · Typography

Sans for strength. Serif for story.

Inter carries the brand. Playfair Display carries the emotion. JetBrains Mono handles labels.

Display · Headlines · UI · Body

Inter

The primary face. Display weights (700–800) for headlines, regular (400–500) for body, semibold (600) for UI and tags. Set tight: -0.025em letter-spacing at display, 0 at body.

400 Regular · 500 Medium 600 Semibold · 700 Bold 800 Extrabold · 900 Black
Chaos into clarity.

Inter is set tight and confident. Body at 17px, line-height 1.6. We use weight contrast — 400 for body, 800 for display — as the system's primary rhythm.

Reserved for emotional weight. Big serif statements on carousels, italic accents inside sans headlines, and editorial pull quotes. Use sparingly — its power comes from contrast with Inter.

05 · The Transformation

The signature pattern.

Every Palmer House message is a transformation: from something broken to something useful. We make that explicit with a chip — strikethrough the old, arrow, name the new in lane color.

We don't make videos.
We turn chaos into clarity.
Posting but nothing moves→Hooks that hold Stiff on camera→Quiet confidence Repeating sales education→Evergreen explainers Tribal knowledge→Internal video library

How to write a transformation

· Old

A real, specific pain

Not abstract. Something a client has actually said out loud. "We answer this question 10 times a week."

· Arrow

→ The move we make

The bridge. Implicit in the arrow: we did the work to close the gap. The arrow is in lane color.

· New

A reusable asset

Concrete, ownable, and ideally something they can show their team Monday morning. Bold, lane color.

Visual treatment · how Pals are rendered

Pal cutouts always sit on a light surface with mix-blend-mode: multiply applied so anti-aliasing doesn't halo. Each Pal carries their lane's "stage" — a soft ellipse of lane color beneath their feet. Never restyle costumes, swap props, recolor, or mix two Pals from different lanes in the same composition.

08 · Carousel System

Social, by template.

Every Pal carousel follows the same skeleton. Same anatomy, same rhythm, swap the lane. This is what makes a feed feel like one brand instead of nine.

Team Alignment

Most teams lose clarity every time work changes hands.

The hidden cost of unclear transitions between people.

Carousel anatomy

  1. Frame. 1080×1350 (4:5 IG portrait) or 1080×1920 (9:16 Story/Reel). Cream background #F4F1EA, never pure white.
  2. Pal badge. Top-right corner. Circular crop of the assigned Pal, 2px lane-color border, multiply blend on white.
  3. Eyebrow. Em-dash + uppercase + lane color. Format: — TEAM ALIGNMENT — · 12px · 0.22em.
  4. Headline. Inter Extrabold OR Playfair Display Bold. One accent word in lane color. Two lines max.
  5. Subhead. Inter Regular, 14–15px, gray. One sentence that earns the headline.
  6. Illustration. 3D-render or watercolor depending on lane. Centered, bottom-aligned, lane-tinted props.
  7. No logo on slide 1. Logo appears only on the cover slide and the end card.

Approved carousel · Team Alignment reference sequence

Slide 01
Hook
Eyebrow + bold question or claim. Pal badge top-right.
Slide 02

Merch rules

One lane per object

A shirt is one lane. A sticker is one lane. The pack is multi-lane — never a single piece.

Monogram for small marks

Anything under 1.5 inches uses the monogram only. Full wordmark for hero placements.

Pals are content characters, not mascots. Apparel uses typography + monogram. Pals live in digital + game contexts.

12 · Do / Don't

The non-negotiables.

If you're unsure about an asset, this page wins. These rules override taste, trend, and personal preference.

Do · ✓
Light base by default

Paper (#FFFFFF) for web. Cream (#F4F1EA) for carousels. Mist (#F6F8FA) for breaks.

Saturated lane color for full bleeds

Section heroes and merch can use full Spotlight, Reel, Evergreen, or System.

Use the transformation chip

Strikethrough old · arrow in lane color · bold new in lane color. Our signature pattern.

Pals on light with multiply blend

Paper or Cream surface, mix-blend-mode: multiply, lane-color stage ellipse below.

One lane per asset

Pick the lane up front. Don't blend lane colors in a single piece.

Eyebrow with em-dashes

— TEAM ALIGNMENT —. Mono uppercase, 0.22em, in lane color.

Don't · ×
No gradients

No linear, radial, mesh, or "subtle" gradients. Flat fills only.

No raw Tailwind colors

Never bg-orange-500. Use the canonical lane tokens.

No claims we haven't earned

No "proven system," no "years of experience." Launch-stage framing only.

No Pal-on-photo composites

Never composite a Pal over a photo or busy texture. Light surface only.

No mixing Pals across lanes

Ryder and Cyrus never appear together. Pals stay in their lane.

No restyled Pals

Never change costumes, swap props, recolor, or generate variations. The cast is fixed.
